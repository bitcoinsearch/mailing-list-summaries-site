Summary:

The email exchanges in this context center around the technicalities of implementing colored coins, including dealing with limitations such as OP_RETURN size and the importance of padding. Flavien Charlon has written a specification document that describes the colored coins protocol being used in the new colored coins web wallet. The context also discusses the implementation of a padded order-based coloring scheme called epobc in coloredcoinlib. Python code for epobc is available on Github, but it is recommended to wait for documentation. In addition, examples of transactions with multiple colored and uncolored outputs, especially p2ptrade transactions, are provided.One suggestion discussed is using different colored coins to represent different numbers of shares, which can be hidden from users with a proper application layer/UI. The protocol should remain as simple as possible, and if a UI can work around the issue, it may not be worth complicating the protocol. Flavien Charlon argues against an alternative proposal of creating different denominations of colored coins for different share amounts, citing the practicality issue of sending fractional amounts. There are debates over whether bitcoins held by a company become part of its capital or are nothing more than a speculative investment owned by the investors, not the company itself. The assets would be listed on the "Asset" section of a company's balance sheet. The conversation also touches on the difference between the colored coin model and Mastercoin model, where the former is linked to transaction outputs while the latter has a notion of address balances. The approach being described is similar to the original colored coins model in most aspects, but OP_RETURN size limitations can pose problems for some applications. The suggestion is made to look into Counterparty or Mastercoin instead.Finally, in an email exchange between Flavien Charlon and Jorge Timón, they debated whether colored coins should be used to pad investments. Flavien argued that colored coins were overblown in importance, while Jorge challenged Flavien's argument, comparing it to printing shares on gold plates. Mark Friedenbach explained that bitcoins owned by a company are nothing more than a speculative investment owned by the investors, not the company itself. The email thread discusses the limitation of OP_RETURN's 40 bytes on the number of colored outputs per transaction, and the author suggests using a compact format (LEB128) for the asset quantity of each output to mitigate this issue.
