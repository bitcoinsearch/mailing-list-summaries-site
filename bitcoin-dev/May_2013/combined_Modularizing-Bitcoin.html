Summary:

The Bitcoin Grant organization is offering a financial incentive of 500 bitcoins or $500,000 for the continued modularization of the Bitcoin system beyond the bloom filter. The aim is to separate the wallet from the node, as mining has already been separated, to create a smarter and more efficient system. This would involve the wallet passing transactions directly to a relay node based on the bloom filter, while the block node maintains the blockchain. The goal is to increase the node count, which currently is fairly low, and prevent cancer attacks.In a discussion on May 16, 2013, Addy Yeow inquired about the number of client nodes on the Bitcoin network. Luke Dashjr confirmed that there were approximately 57,000 nodes running protocol >= 70001 with a timestamp no older than 24 hours. However, an email from May 16, 2013 suggested that estimates at the time put the node count around 8,000. The conversation also mentioned that modularization could strengthen the system and increase the node count, potentially making cancer attacks less likely.The Bitcoin Grant initiative is offering a financial incentive of 500 bitcoins or $500,000 for continued modularization, with a focus on separating the wallet from the node to prevent cancer attacks and increase the node count. Grant proposals and feedback are welcome. One proposal suggests defining protocols and APIs of various modules to promote diversity of implementations and ease optimization work.
