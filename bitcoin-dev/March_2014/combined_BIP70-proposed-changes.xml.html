Summary:

The Bitcoin community is discussing proposed changes to the Bitcoin Improvement Proposal (BIP) 70 payment protocol. One proposal includes requiring certificates to contain the first certificate plus all intermediate certificates but not the root certificate to enable offline verification. Another suggestion is that times in the payment protocol should be required to be in UTC rather than local time zone. Ryan X. Charles has suggested implementing merge avoidance within BitPay to increase customer and merchant privacy. He also highlighted that there is no concept of the status of a payment after it has been made in the payment protocol. Charles expressed interest in working with the community to establish a complete, decentralised authentication protocol and BitPay is working on a new standard based on bitcoin-like addresses for authentication. It has also proposed BIP 73 for shorter URIs and less dense QR codes. In an email conversation, Charles suggested designing a protocol extension to allow merchants to delegate their signature authority to BitPay.
