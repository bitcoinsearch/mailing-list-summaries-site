Summary:

The Bitcoin developer community is currently debating the use of SIGHASH_ANYPREVOUT (APO) instead of CTV's proposed covenant design. The need for stable transaction IDs, particularly for use cases like channel factories, is important, but quantum computing concerns may make APO less preferred for long-term multi-decade vault storage. Some members who have been trying to implement practical vaults for the past two years doubt that CTV is necessary or sufficient for this purpose, believing that APO-AS covers it. APO-AS covenants can cover CTV's features and can be considered as an optimization of CTV. However, the implementation of APO may not be as widely accepted as CTV's.The author of a post on the bitcoin-dev mailing list suggests replacing BIP119 with a slightly modified version of BIP118 that can emulate CTV with optional "ANYONECANPAY" behavior, which they believe could benefit most Bitcoin users due to interest in both simple covenants and better off-chain protocols. Additionally, there is discussion about byte-efficiency in APO-AS for potential vault users and the deployment of new applications such as Eltoo and blind statechains, which can be leveraged using ANYPREVOUT.There is also debate over whether activating CTV over APO to avoid quantum computer fears is fair. The email thread debates the use of BIP118 as an alternative to BIP119, with some opposing it due to lack of documentation and testing tools.Overall, while there are concerns about the long-term storage of bitcoins in non-quantum-computer vulnerable bare-CTV-covenants, there is ongoing debate within the Bitcoin developer community regarding the implementation of APO and CTV covenants. Some believe that APO-AS covers all the use cases for vaults, and that potential use-case applications of APO should be explored while waiting for on-chain usage of a less efficient construction to achieve the same goal. Meanwhile, BIP118 is being considered as a soft fork candidate that could benefit more Bitcoin users, given the interest in simple covenants and better off-chain protocols.The Bitcoin Improvement Proposal (BIP) 118 proposes the use of a new opcode called APOAS, which would enhance Bitcoin's Script language by adding more flexibility to its covenants. The APOAS feature grants protocol designers more flexibility than what is currently possible with CTV covenants. Users can compose covenant transactions with other SIGHASH modes and re-bind them to different UTXOs.Although some members of the Bitcoin community have expressed concerns about the safety of BIP-118, the author of the proposal believes that the ongoing debate is part of the maturation process of the protocol and welcomes it. The email thread discusses the benefits and drawbacks of using APO instead of CTV, including txid stability and higher witness satisfaction costs. The author acknowledges the benefits of using APO in the short-term but believes that CTV provides more flexibility in the long-term. Overall, the discussion revolves around finding the best solution for implementing covenants in Bitcoin while balancing safety and flexibility concerns. The proposal offers greater flexibility to the users and may lead to further improvements in the future.
