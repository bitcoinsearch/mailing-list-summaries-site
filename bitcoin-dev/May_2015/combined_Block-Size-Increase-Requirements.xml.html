The discussion surrounding Bitcoin's block size increase is a complex and ongoing issue. Various proposals have been suggested, including a miner flag that would allow miners to produce smaller blocks than general users and merchants. However, concerns have been raised about the potential for denial-of-service attacks on miners. Other suggestions include tracking both miner and non-miner chain tips and rejecting 4MB blocks when in "miner mode." The issue of centralization and the potential risks associated with block size increases has also been raised, as well as the need for long-term fee incentives and better conclusions.There are various opinions on what the appropriate size for Bitcoin blocks should be. Some argue that linear growth is the safest option, as it models diminishing returns better and has a smaller risk of growing too much. Others believe that increasing block size may lead to centralization and discourage scaling technologies from being developed. Additionally, there are concerns about the impact of bigger blocks on mining and validation centralization and how to ensure miners don't cheat and stop validating blocks fully before building on them.The discussion also involves long-term incentives within the system and the potential use of hashing assurance contracts (HACs) to fund network security. In addition, there are concerns about discouraging scaling technologies from being developed if the block size is increased too much. The debate over block size increase has resulted in various proposals, including increasing it by 1 MB per year or having a dynamic limit that scales with average block size.In an email exchange between Gavin Andresen and Chun Wang from F2Pool, they discuss the possibility of bad miners attacking the network with artificial big blocks. F2Pool suggests a smooth increase in the max block size, starting with 2 MB first, while Matt Corallo argues for better technologies such as those that decrease block propagation latency. There are also concerns about long-term incentives within the system and mining centralization.
